Here's the latest from the Daily Record.

A man has been arrested in Glasgow by Northern Irish police, with help from Police Scotland, on suspicion of fraudulently importing a class B controlled drug. Detectives from the Police Service of Northern Ireland’s organised crime branch led the operation.

Meanwhile, MSPs are set to vote next week on scrapping Scotland’s controversial “not proven” verdict, which currently gives juries a third option beyond guilty or not guilty. Justice Minister Angela Constance says abolishing the three-verdict system will be a top priority if the bill passes.
